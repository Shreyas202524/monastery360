The Hermit's Cell Above Sikkim's First Capital

Perched on a forested hill above Yuksom, Dubdi Monastery stands as Sikkim's oldest and most historically significant monastery. Founded in 1701 by Lhatsun Namkha Jigme, one of the three revered lamas who crowned Sikkim's first king, this ancient retreat offers a sacred pilgrimage through dense forests to a sanctuary of profound tranquility and spiritual wisdom.

Founding and Historical Significance

The history of Dubdi is inextricably linked to the birth of the Kingdom of Sikkim. Its name, which translates to "the retreat" or "the hermit's cell," aptly captures the profound tranquility and spiritual seclusion that has defined it for over three centuries.

üëë Birth of the Kingdom

Following the coronation of Phuntsog Namgyal as the first Chogyal (king) of Sikkim in 1642 by three revered lamas, one of them, Lhatsun Namkha Jigme, founded Dubdi Monastery in 1701.

üëë

Royal Foundation

Established by Lhatsun Namkha Jigme, one of the three revered lamas who coronated Sikkim's first king in 1642.

Nyingma Sect Center

Location and Trek

Located at an altitude of about 2,100 meters (7,000 feet), reaching Dubdi is a pilgrimage in itself.

ü•æ Sacred Pilgrimage Trek

The monastery is accessible via a steep, approximately 3-kilometer (1.9-mile) trek from Yuksom, which takes about an hour.

The path winds through a dense, verdant forest of oak and chestnut, offering a serene and picturesque journey with spectacular views of the Himalayan peaks, including the majestic Kanchenjunga range.

Architecture and Sacred Art

Dubdi Monastery is a beautiful, two-storied stone structure that exemplifies traditional Sikkimese monastic architecture. Though modest in size, its interior is a treasure trove of religious art.
