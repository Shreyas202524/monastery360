Royal Foundation and Sacred Legacy

Ralong Monastery's story is deeply intertwined with the travels of the Karmapas and the royal patronage of Sikkim's Chogyals, creating a magnificent spiritual center that bridges ancient traditions with contemporary Buddhist practice.

👑 Royal Promise Fulfilled by 4th Chogyal

In 1768, the 4th Chogyal Gyurmed Namgyal founded the original monastery in fulfillment of a sacred promise to the 9th Karmapa, who blessed this site upon his return from Tibet pilgrimage.

This royal patronage established Ralang as a premier center for Kagyu Buddhist learning and practice in South Sikkim.

🏛️ Two Sacred Complexes: Old & New

Visitors encounter both the original Old Ralong Monastery (1768) with its ancient spiritual aura, and the magnificent New Ralong Monastery (Palchen Choeling Institute), consecrated by the 12th Gyaltsab Rinpoche in 1995.

Architectural Marvel and Sacred Art Collection

The New Ralong Monastery stands as an architectural wonder built in traditional Tibetan style, housing one of Sikkim's most extensive and exquisite collections of sacred Buddhist art.

🎨 Exquisite Thangkas & Sacred Murals

The monastery is renowned for its extensive collection of thangkas (religious painted scrolls), statues, and vibrant murals that adorn prayer halls and chambers. The intricate frescoes beautifully depict the life of Buddha and the lineage of the Karmapas.

Spectacular Sacred Festivals

Ralong Monastery is famous for two major annual festivals that attract thousands of devotees and tourists, showcasing the vibrant cultural heritage of Sikkim and Tibetan Buddhism.

🏔️ Pang Lhabsol: Mount Kanchenjunga Festival

This unique Sikkimese festival (August/September) venerates Mount Kanchenjunga as the guardian deity of Sikkim and commemorates the blood brotherhood pact between Lepcha and Bhutia communities. Features spectacular warrior dances by monks.

A powerful celebration of Sikkim's cultural unity and spiritual heritage performed with great fervor at Ralong.

🎭 Mahakala Chaam: Sacred Masked Dances

Held in the tenth month of the Tibetan calendar (December), this vibrant festival features elaborate masked dances (Chaam) performed by monks, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil in a powerful spiritual spectacle.

⚔️

Warrior Dance Performances

Spectacular warrior dances during Pang Lhabsol festival, celebrating the unity between Lepcha and Bhutia communities of Sikkim.

🎭

Sacred Masked Chaam

Elaborate masked dances during Mahakala Chaam festival, symbolizing victory of good over evil through powerful spiritual performances.

🙏

Cultural Heritage Celebration

Both festivals attract multitudes of devotees and tourists, preserving and celebrating living traditions of Tibetan Buddhism.
