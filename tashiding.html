A Legendary Founding and Sacred History

Tashiding's history is steeped in legend and sacred lore, deeply connected to the introduction of Buddhism in Sikkim. The monastery is a nexus of Sikkimese faith, history, and breathtaking natural beauty.

üéØ Guru Padmasambhava's Arrow

It is believed that the 8th-century Buddhist master Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) shot an arrow into the air, vowing to meditate where it landed.

The arrow fell on the very hill where the monastery now stands, consecrating the land as sacred ground.

üéØ

Sacred Consecration

The hilltop was blessed by Guru Padmasambhava in the 8th century, making it one of the most sacred sites in Sikkim.

üëë

Founded in 1641

Formally established by Ngadak Sempa Chempo, one of the three lamas who consecrated Sikkim's first king.

Architectural and Spiritual Highlights

The monastery complex is a classic example of traditional Sikkimese monastic architecture, creating an atmosphere of deep serenity and devotion.

The Bhumchu Festival: A Sacred Water Ceremony

Tashiding is most famous for the Bhumchu Festival, one of the most sacred and significant religious events in Sikkim.

üåä Sacred Water Prophecy

Held annually on the 14th and 15th days of the first month of the Tibetan lunar calendar (usually February or March), drawing thousands of devotees from across the Himalayan region.

The festival centers on opening a sacred pot containing holy water blessed by Guru Padmasambhava himself - the water's level and quality predict Sikkim's fortunes for the coming year.
